Watching Mr.Sharif Mousallati LenoGulf chairman in Kano Nigeria ever since I can remember managing the commodities business. I was always intrigued to watch him dealing with farmers, (countless women sieve sesame), pick hibiscus flower, and sundry ginger. I wanted to follow his footsteps. I would watch the hardships of getting the commodities to our clients and the countless obstacles in each process of the trade. I always admired how much passion, endurance, patience, and dedication it took to trade and process commodities from farmer to end user..But what always lingered in the back of my mind is “how can we add value to our organisation, “how can we keep the farmer happy and the customer happy alike”, “what could be done to differentiate ourselves from the competition”. Having the opportunity to get a 1st class education in a 1st class country with work experience in both Sudan and Nigeria it was my goal to combine the best of both (world). The aim was to benefit our farmers, suppliers, organisation, team, and clients with an overall product/service superior to the norm. With all the above in mind, we redefined our strategy with our goals clearly identified into categories: supply, processing, logistics, and trading. We knew we had to identify and empower/enrich our premium farmers and supplier. Then upgrade our processing lines to include the best technology available immediately and upgrade according to international norms yearly. Logistics is always an obstacle that causes delays and frustration to us and our clients. So, we partnered up with pioneers (in land) logistics to increase our success in delivery time in receiving the commodities and similarly delivering the processed commodities to the port terminals. We also made vivid and outlined agreements with shipping companies to forecast our demand and allow them to supplier empty containers and booking priority. Finally, for the trading, we realised we need to be in a hub that facilitated the financial cycles and increase the confidence due to first-class banks and modern banking service. So we opened our company head office in the UAE and opened our company bank accounts with 1st class banks. However, we did not stop here, we continue to enhance the above processes with international accreditations, and certifications. We also have a solid long-term vision with concrete steps to grow the business to further our reach to more demanding clients. Our current expansions involve adding value to the commodities we trade. The aim is to empower our existing clients rather than compete with them. This is being done by further processing the commodities to allow us to send quality “added value” products to your existing clients so we both grow together. Finally, with the increase dependance on technology and automation, we have also partnered up with digital pioneers in Germany to build our private trading platform to connect our certified suppliers, and buyers.

Sesame Seeds
Sesame seeds (is) a flowering plant. Tiny, (oil rich) seeds that grow in pods on the Seasamum indicum plant. They are one of the oldest oilseeds crops known, domesticated well over 3000 years ago. The plant is mainly grown in Africa and is highly tolerant to drought-like conditions. The seeds are consumed internationally, (un hulled) hulled, crushed, and pressed. It is used whole in cooking for its rich, nutty flavour, hulled sesame used on (breads) such as burger buns and bagels. Asian Cousine has ample usage of the seed and oil form ranging from garnishing on sushi, salads, dim sum, and sesame seed balls. While middle east Cousine crushes the sesame to a past (know) as Tahini used in many recipes such as falafel, humous, and baked beans.
The seed is a great source of vitamins B, proteins, and fibre. It’s also known to reduce blood pressure, and support healthy bones.
Hibiscus Flower
Hibiscus Flower also known as Rose Mallow, Hardy Hibiscus, Rose of Sharon, and Roselle…
This flower is native to warm, subtropical, and tropical regions worldwide. Hibiscus flower is handpicked carefully to preserve the flower, and sun-dried. The vibrant red colour and unique taste adds colour to teas, soft drinks, and beverages. It is also used as food ingredients in dished native to Australia, Burma, and India.
The flower is packed with antioxidants, known to assist lower blood pressure, boost liver health, and help fight bacteria.
Watermelon Seeds
Watermelon plants are addicted to heat and require (long) warm growing season of at least 70-90 days. They are native and thrive in both humid and semi-arid environments. The seeds are removed from the (water melon) fruit, and depending on the process the seed can also be extracted from the casing. The seed is consumed worldwide,however, the Asian, and Middle-eastern cousins have this seed deeply engraved in their cultures. The seed consumption ranges from snacks, salad garnishing, soups, paste, and is further processed to oils.
The seeds are nutrient-dense, rich in vitamins, and high in calories.
Dried Ginger Flakes
Ginger or Ginger root is in the family Zingiberaceae, is mostly produced in India, Nigeria, China, and Indonesia. The root is extracted, sliced, and sun-dried. It is widely used as a spice and in medicine worldwide. The tangy taste, strong aroma, and hot/spicy flavour is sharp and refreshing. These characteristics makes it the key ingredient in Indian, and Asian cousin… Also, it is consumed worldwide in teas, energy drinks, and herbal medicines.
The benefits besides its dominant taste range from reduction in risk diabetes, anti-inflammatory, and help prevent heart disease.
Chickpeas
Chickpeas is also known as Egyptian pea, garbanzo bean, and Deli Chana. They are a type of pulse cultivated mostly in India, Ethiopia, Sudan, Mexico, Iran, and Turkey. The Chickpeas is consumed in a variety of ways after cooking, ranging from cold in salads, cooked in stews, ground into flour to make falafel, Indian dishes, and they are the main ingredient for Hummus.
The Chickpeas is a nutrient-dense food providing a rich source of vitamins, minerals, and fibre. They are known to improve digestion and they make an excellent replacement for meat in vegetarian and vegan dishes.
Peanuts
Peanuts also (known)as Groundnuts is a legume crop grown mainly for its edible seeds. It is widely grown in tropics, and (sub tropics). The peanut pods develop underground. The nut requires warm weather throughout the growing conditions and require 85-130 before harvesting. Nigeria, Sudan, China, Myanmar, and the US are major producers worldwide. The nut is commonly consumed world wide salted and roasted. Boiled peanuts are a popular snack in India, China, and (US). Peanut oil is common for cooking, and most importantly peanut butter made from (grounding) dry roasted peanuts.
The nut is full of healthy fats, proteins, and fibre. They are nutrient rich and low in carbohydrates.
Stone Flower
Stone Flower is an edible lichen flora (a type of fungus), which grows on trees, rocks and stones generally. It is used as a spice to enhance the flavor and taste of food.
It has a strong woody aroma and flavor.
For better results it should be roasted in a little oil to release its full aroma.
Stone flower is an antiseptic, Pain killer, anti-fungal and antibiotic. It also helps manage skin problems, astringent and anti-viral.
